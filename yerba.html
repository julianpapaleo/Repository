The plant

Yerba mate or yerba-maté is a plant species native to
South America. It was named by the French botanist
Augustin Saint-Hilaire. The leaves of the plant can be
steeped in hot water to make a beverage known as mate.
Brewed cold, it is used to make tereré. Both the plant and
the beverage contain caffeine.
